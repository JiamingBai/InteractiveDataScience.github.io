Exploratory Analysis of Austin Animal Shelter

Jiaming Bai & Fan Qian

Overview:

Austin Animal Center Shelter is the largest no-kill animal shelter in the United States that 16,000 animals each year and animal protection and pet resource services to all of Austin and Travis County. The organization accepts lost and surrendered animals from all of Travis Country and aim to find forever homes for them.

Breed:

We were curious about the relationship between breeds and different outcomes. Perhaps some breeds are more popular for adoption and some are not. Thus we created this graph that shows the outcome for top 15 breeds in the data. You can see in the graph that domestic shorthair has a lot more records than other breeds, in fact, it makes up 81% of the total population and fortunately most of them are transferred or adopted.

Age against outcome:

Another factor that may affect the outcome of the animal could be its age. For example, we hypothesized that baby animals might have a greater chance of dying since they are weaker physically. From this interactive visualization, you can feel to select any value of outcome or a combination of some outcomes to see what are the ages of these animals.

Most popular names:

Just for fun, we just wanted to see what are some popular animal names and here are the distributions. The different colors in a bar denote different animal types as marked by the legend.
