About this data set

The dataset mainly records the outcome of the accepted animals and general information about them such as breed, age and color.

Data Cleaning:

First, we used python scripts to separate column “sex upon outcome” to columns of sex(female,male) and outcome(neutered,spayed,intact), and separate rows with unknown “sex upon outcome” will have unknown for both sex and outcome.

Second, we found there are duplicated animal_ids, thus we used Microsoft Excel to remove duplicate ones and only keep the first occurance.

In the original dataset, the breed of the animal are carefully documented and some of them are too detailed that we will end up with a lot of categories for breed. Therefore, we created a new column called dominant breed to narrow down the number of types of breeds in the data.

Where did this data come from?

This dataset was found on Kaggle and is publicly available for downloadhere.

Outcome types and subtypes

    Adoption: the animal was adopted to a home
    Barn Adoption: the animal was adopted to live in a barn
    Offsite Missing: the animal went missing for unknown reasons at a partner offsite location
    In-Foster Missing: the animal is missing after being placed in a foster home
    In-Kennel Missing: the animal is missing after being transferred to a kennel facility
    Possible Theft: Although not confirmed, the animal went missing as a result of theft from facility
    Barn Transfer: The animal was transferred to a facility for adoption into a barn environment
    SNR: SNR refers to the city of Austin's Shelter-Neuter-Release program. I believe the outcome is representative of the animal being released.
