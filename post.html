Data Quality

Data accountablility:

This dataset directly comes from the Austin Animal Center Shelter. As part of the City of Austin Open Data Initiative, the Austin Animal Center makes available its collected dataset that contains statistics and outcomes of animals entering the Austin Animal Services system. Therefore, we think it is safe to conclude that this data is accountable.

Data coherency:

Overall the data is relatively coherent. Each column has their appropriate values and the categorical values are consistent throughout the dataset. One thing that we noticed during data cleaning is that there are duplicate values for animal_id. After we took a closer look, most of the information across duplicate rows are the same, except age_upon_outcome. For example: one row would say 6 years and another says 7 years. We decided to keep the first occurrence of the id and maintain the uniqueness of animal_id.

Data completeness:

The dataset contains shelter outcomes of several types of animals and breeds from 10/1/2013 to 9 months ago. The data has no missing values in the column of outcome, therefore, at least every animal in the dataset has a recorded outcome. There are some missing values in name and outcome_subtype, but since these are not our main focus of analysis, we donâ€™t think these missing values compromise the completeness of data.

Data correctness:

We think the data makes sense in many ways. For example: most animals that had rabies and died are bats. The animal types in the dataset also seem correct because domestic shorthair is a common breed across the country.
